Bio (Age and Ethnicity) – Who is Hauntzer?

Even though in the gaming world the player is popular as Hauntzer, his real name is Kevin Yarnell. A professional League of Legends actor, he was born on May 20, 1995 in the United States. Hauntzer is an American of Chinese/American ethnicity.

That said, he has managed to keep everything about his parents and siblings, if he has any, away from the media. More so, its educational status is not known as information about it is also kept away from the public domain by the first tape.

He was raised in Seattle, Washington, where he started playing video games at an early age. For League of Legends, which would later make him famous, he started his career while still in high school.

Professionally, Hauntzer started his career with The Gentlemen’s Guild in 2013, but after just two months, he joined the LoLPro team where he spent less than a year. His next team was Curse Academy, where he stayed for a few months, then he signed for Gravity Gaming from January to November 2015. It was from there that he moved on to Team SoloMid.

In 2018, Hauntzer made a rather shocking move from TSM to the team he now plays for, Golden Guardians. The transfer didn’t just shock fans and followers of the sport; it was also up to Kevin who revealed that he had not been given notice of the transfer in progress or a provision allowing him to share the starting points he had lost.

During his three years with the organization, he proved to be even better than the good player everyone expected him to be. Even when the team had a very difficult season in 2018, he was still in sight. Nonetheless, he was later dropped from the starting lineup for Sergen “Broken Blade” Çelik. With the team, he won three NA LCS playoff titles.

With GGS, he again secured the top spot among top laners, alongside big names such as Kim “Olleh” Joo-sung, Henrik “Froggen” Hansen, and Juan “Contractz” Garcia.

Talking about his move, the prolific top lanerEven though he didn’t think it was the wisest move from his former team, it was still a good opportunity for him as it allowed him to be in a better situation.
