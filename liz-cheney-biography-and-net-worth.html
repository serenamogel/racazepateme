Biography

Liz Cheney, the eldest of Dick and Lynne Cheney’s two children, was born on July 28, 1966 in Madison, Wisconsin. Mary is her sister. The family split its time between Casper, Wyoming, and Washington, DC, after Dick was chosen to serve in the US Congress in the 1970s. Cheney attended McLean High School as an adolescent, where she earned her diploma in 1984. She continued her education by enrolling in Colorado College and the University of Chicago Law School, where she graduated with a Juris Doctor in 1996.

Career

Cheney spent five years working for the US Agency for International Development and the State Department prior to attending law school. She then started working at Armitage Associates, a consulting company. Following her completion of law school, Cheney worked as an international law attorney and consultant at the International Finance Corporation in addition to practicing law at the firm White & Case. She also worked as a USAID official at the American embassies in Warsaw, Poland, and Budapest, Hungary.

Political Career

During the administration of President George W. Bush, Liz worked as the main deputy assistant secretary of state for Near Eastern affairs from 2003 to 2004. Liz Cheney, a challenger to current U.S. Senator Mike Enzi, declared her candidacy for the Wyoming U.S. Senate seat in 2013.

After receiving harsh criticism from several Republicans for her prior support for same-sex marriage and abortion rights, she finally withdrew from the campaign. Liz Cheney was chosen to serve in the at-large congressional district for Wyoming in the 2016 election. She then ran for reelection to keep her position and is now a member of the Energy and Commerce Committee.
